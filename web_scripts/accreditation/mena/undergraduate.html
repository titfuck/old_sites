Undergraduate Response

 

  1. 1.Theta Delta Chi should complete a membership review of current undergraduate members in order to be certain to begin the 2008-2009 academic year with a strong, stable membership committed to the ideals, values and expectations of membership in Theta Delta Chi. This process is suggested to be a partnership between the undergraduate chapter leadership, alumni volunteers, and potentially the Theta Delta Chi Inter/National Office.

  2. The undergraduates of Theta Delta Chi underwent a brotherhood review from the alumni at the end of the Spring 2008.  It was decided that all members are dedicated to the betterment of Theta Delta Chi and to make sure that this happens, a points system monitoring all members was ratified following a house meeting on October 27, 2008.  The points system will be sent to the FSILG office for further review.

  3. Overall, the brotherhood response so far this semester has been excellent.  Participation has increased and Brothers are taking a more active role in their respective offices and helping improve our fraternity.  Participation in all types of brotherhood activities are up and morale is high

  4. Extras:  Please see the Brotherhood Review Questionnaire and the latest iteration of the TDC Points System.

  1. 2.A measurable, realistic, and comprehensive plan for Year-Round recruitment of new brothers should be written and implemented beginning Fall 2008. Written plans must be submitted to and reviewed by the FSILG Office staff each semester for Fall 2008, Spring 2009, Fall 2009, and Spring 2010.

  2. The Brotherhood has in fact been very active on the year-round recruitment front.  We have maintained contact with all potential new members that did not join during formal recruitment in order to form the nucleus of a spring pledge class.  We have developed new contacts through social events designed with MIT freshmen in mind and have encouraged new members to bring freshmen to TDC.  This plan is in accord with the lessons learned from the Recruitment workshop at TDC in late August.  Please look at the “Extras” section below for the Spring 2009 recruitment strategy.

  3. Extras:  Please see the TDC Recruitment Strategy for Spring 2009.

  1. 3.The undergraduate leadership should consider a recruitment skill-building workshop to further educate and prepare members for a shift in recruitment tactics. Many fraternity and sorority headquarters and speaker bureaus provide best practice ideas. One workshop resource to consider is CampusSpeak’s “Recruitment Boot Camp”. More information can be found at http://www.campuspeak.com/programs/bootcamp/chapter.html. The FSILG Office would split the cost of this program with the chapter.

  2. The fall recruitment chairs invited Eric Kelley, the Theta Delta Chi International Fraternity Director of Expansion for a half-day “Recruitment Boot Camp” workshop with the Brotherhood.  A lot of the information Brother Kelley provided us was extremely useful and we believe that without it, we would not have been able to recruit as well as we did.

  3. Per AILG and TDC’s House Corporation request, we plan on having next year’s recruitment chair (Benjamin Williams ’11) attend the recruitment programs recommended by the FSILG office for next year’s formal recruitment period.  The hope is that these programs will aid in changing the culture of recruitment around TDC.

  2. The parlor fee has been charged to non-resident Brothers and the fee is currently set to $400 per semester but this may change next semester.

  1. 5.It is strongly suggested that the chapter clearly expect and enforce that all undergraduate officers live in the house.

  2. Starting with the next round of officers who are elected in February, all officers will be required to live in house for the duration of the term of office, otherwise immediate resignation from office is required.  This is further reflected in the current points system by means of a significant points loss.

  3. Extras:  Please see the TDC Points System for more information.

  1. 6.It is strongly suggested that expectations and obligations for upperclass in-house residency be explained and enforced to all potential new members and classes.

  2. The new “Brotherhood Code of Honor” that all Brothers are required to sign indicates that they have to be responsible for living up to the ideals of our Fraternity and this will be enforced via the points system.  New members are given their expectations via the Bid Speech given to them before they become a part of the Fraternity.

  3. Furthermore, there is significant pressure on undergrads from both the alumni and undergrads alike to live in the house and our policies reflect this.  Negative reinforcement of this expectation is in place via the points system and its associated scholarships.  A heavy point loss is given to those who choose not to live at the house as well as a loss in eligibility for the scholarships given out by the Alumni, as per their recommendations.

  4. Extras:  Please see the TDC Points System and the Brotherhood Code of Honor.
